Bali's Most Iconic Attractions

Hand-picked by our local experts — these are the unmissable experiences every Bali visitor should have.

Temple All Ages

Tanah Lot Temple

Tabanan Regency, West Bali

One of Bali's most iconic landmarks, Tanah Lot is a 16th-century sea temple dramatically perched on a rocky islet. Best visited at sunset when the temple silhouettes against a blazing sky — a truly magical sight. The temple is especially sacred during Balinese sea turtle ceremonies.

2–3 hrs ~$5

Nature Easy

Ubud Monkey Forest

Ubud, Gianyar Regency

A sacred Balinese Hindu sanctuary home to over 1,000 Balinese long-tailed macaques, set in a dense ancient forest with moss-covered stone temples. An extraordinary place to observe wildlife in a spiritual setting, with three main temples dating back to the 14th century.

1.5–2 hrs ~$8
★★★★★ 4.7
Learn More →
Adventure Moderate

Mount Batur

Kintamani, Bangli Regency

An active stratovolcano rising 1,717m above sea level, Mount Batur offers Bali's most thrilling sunrise trek. Depart at 3am to reach the summit by dawn and watch the sun rise above a sea of clouds over the vast volcanic caldera. The views are absolutely breathtaking.

Full Day ~$35
★★★★★ 4.9

Beach Easy

Seminyak Beach

Seminyak, Badung Regency

Bali's most stylish beach strip stretching for miles of golden sand, lined with chic beach clubs like Ku De Ta and Potato Head. Perfect for sunset cocktails, casual beach dining, and gentle swimming. The legendary double six sunset is not to be missed by any visitor.

Half Day Free
★★★★★ 4.8
Learn More →
Culture Easy

Tegalalang Rice Terraces

Tegalalang, Gianyar Regency

Bali's most photographed landscape — sweeping terraced rice paddies sculpted into steep hillsides using the traditional Subak irrigation system, a UNESCO World Heritage practice. Walk among the paddies at dawn when the light is soft and mist rises from the valley below.

2 hrs ~$3
★★★★☆ 4.7
Learn More →
Temple Easy

Uluwatu Temple

Pecatu, Badung Regency

Perched 70 metres above the crashing Indian Ocean on sheer limestone cliffs, Pura Luhur Uluwatu is one of Bali's six directional temples. The nightly Kecak fire dance performed at sunset against the ocean backdrop is one of the most spectacular cultural shows in Asia.

2–3 hrs ~$8
