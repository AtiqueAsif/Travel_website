Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers to the questions we hear most often from travellers planning their Bali trip.

How far in advance should I book my Bali trip?

For peak season (July–August and December–January), we recommend booking at least 3–4 months in advance — especially for popular hotels and temple tours. For shoulder season (May–June, September–October), 6–8 weeks is usually sufficient. Last-minute bookings are possible but availability and prices are less favourable.

What is included in a guided tour package?

All our guided tour packages include a dedicated local driver-guide, private air-conditioned vehicle, entrance fees to all attractions on the itinerary, a welcome breakfast at a local warung, and 24/7 WhatsApp support throughout your trip. Hotel accommodation, meals, and flights are typically booked separately unless you choose our full-package option.

Is Bali suitable for solo female travellers?

Absolutely — Bali is consistently rated one of the safest and most welcoming destinations in Southeast Asia for solo female travellers. Ubud in particular has a thriving community of solo female digital nomads and wellness retreaters. Standard precautions apply: avoid walking alone late at night in isolated areas, and always trust your instincts. Our team can connect solo travellers with small-group tours for a social experience with added security.

Do I need a visa to visit Bali?

Most Western nationalities (UK, EU, USA, Australia, Canada, etc.) can enter Indonesia visa-free for 30 days, or obtain a Visa on Arrival (VOA) at Ngurah Rai Airport for approximately USD $32. The VOA is extendable once for an additional 30 days. Always check the current Indonesian immigration website before travelling as policies can change. If in doubt, contact us — we check visa requirements for every guest as part of our trip planning service.

What currency should I bring to Bali?

The local currency is Indonesian Rupiah (IDR). While USD, AUD, EUR, and GBP are widely accepted at hotels and larger restaurants, you'll get better value exchanging to Rupiah at authorised money changers. We recommend bringing USD $200–400 in cash as a backup and relying primarily on ATM withdrawals, which are plentiful throughout tourist areas. Avoid exchanging money with street touts.
