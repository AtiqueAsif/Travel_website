Taste the Flavours of Bali

From smoky roadside satay to ceremonial feasts — Balinese cuisine is a journey for all the senses

About Balinese Food

A Cuisine Shaped by Temple & Tradition

Balinese cuisine is a vibrant tapestry woven from Hindu ritual, fertile volcanic soil, and centuries of spice trade influence. Unlike most Indonesian cooking, Balinese food is not just sustenance — it is ceremony. Offerings of food are presented to the gods multiple times daily, and certain dishes are reserved exclusively for religious celebrations.

The cuisine is defined by its base gede — a complex spice paste of shallots, garlic, galangal, turmeric, lemongrass, candlenuts, and more — pounded fresh each day. Coconut is used in every form: oil, milk, grated flesh, and sugar. The heat comes from a generous hand with fresh bird's eye chillies.

Did you know? Babi Guling (suckling pig) is considered a sacred dish in Bali, historically prepared only for religious ceremonies. Today it's also served in warungs, but the ceremony remains deeply spiritual.

Must Try

Essential Balinese Dishes

Six iconic dishes that define the flavours of Bali — each one a window into the island's rich culinary heritage.

Nasi Goreng

Indonesian Fried Rice

Bali's most beloved comfort food — wok-tossed day-old rice with shallots, garlic, sweet soy sauce, chilli, and topped with a perfectly fried egg. Every warung does it differently, but it's always deeply satisfying.

Spice: Medium
$5 – $8
Try this →

Babi Guling

Balinese Suckling Pig

The crown jewel of Balinese cuisine — a whole young pig stuffed with a spice paste of turmeric, lemongrass, and galangal, then slowly rotated over a wood fire for hours until the skin is glass-crisp and golden. A cultural institution.

Spice: Hot
$8 – $12
Try this →

Sate Lilit

Balinese Minced Satay

Unlike mainland satay, Sate Lilit is made by wrapping spiced minced fish or chicken around thick lemongrass stalks, then grilling over coconut-husk coals. The result is incredibly fragrant, juicy, and aromatic — a true Balinese speciality.

Spice: Mild
$4 – $7
Try this →

Bebek Betutu

Slow-Roasted Stuffed Duck

A ceremonial Balinese dish of whole duck packed with base gede spice paste and cassava leaves, wrapped in banana leaves, then slow-cooked in rice husks for up to 12 hours. The result is impossibly tender, fragrant, and deeply complex in flavour.

Spice: Medium
$12 – $18
Try this →

Lawar

Balinese Mixed Vegetable Salad

A traditional Balinese salad of finely chopped vegetables, minced meat or fish, grated coconut, and an aromatic spice mix. Lawar is central to every Balinese feast and ceremony. Try Lawar Putih (white lawar) for a lighter, vegetarian version.

Spice: Medium
$3 – $5
Try this →

Dadar Gulung

Pandan Coconut Crêpe

Bali's most beautiful dessert — thin, vivid-green pandan-flavoured crêpes rolled around a sweet filling of grated coconut and palm sugar (gula Bali). A delicately sweet, fragrant treat sold at every traditional market and a perfect conclusion to any Balinese meal.

Spice: Sweet
$2 – $4

After Dark

Explore Bali's Night Markets

Bali's pasar malam (night markets) are where the real culinary magic happens. Dozens of vendors set up under string lights, selling everything from crispy martabak to fragrant nasi campur. The Gianyar Night Market is legendary — locals and travellers queue together for the same outstanding food at street prices.
